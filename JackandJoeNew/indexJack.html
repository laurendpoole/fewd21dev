Jack Barton Poole was born on December 18, 1925 in Abingdon, Virginia. In 1927 he became an older brother to Charles Lindbergh "Lindy" Poole and again in 1931 to William Carlos Poole. Jack loved drawing at an early age and at 8 years old, he received an orange and colored pencils for christmas. These were his only gifts from Santa that year.

BIRDS

Jack continued with drawing and took art classes at the University of Louisville during his college years. He eventually settled down and married Vivian Virginia Deeds. They had two sons and three daughters together.

FLOWERS

Vivian loved Emmitt Kelly, who was a sad faced clown in the 50's, and many of Jack’s drawings for Vivian were modeled after that style. One of his grandsons, Greg, even drew a sad faced clown as his first school drawing.

Jack drew a daily advertisement in the local paper, “Do you think you can draw?”. However, he did not like to draw or paint in 3 dimensions.

BUTTERFLIES

Jack always kept newly sharpened pencils on the table next to his chair which no one was allowed to use except for his young children and grandchildren who sat with him.

Jack Barton Poole died on October 19, 1989, in DC. He was 63 years old. Much of his artistic ability was passed on to his 5 children, 12 grandchildren, and 25 great grandchildren.
